Local earthquake locations and origin times from HRSN

Below is the catalog of 256 HRSN locations for events with epicenters occurring within 5 km of the M2 target sequences for Oct. 15 through Nov. 30, 2003. In the reference frame of our velocity models the target epicenters are located at approx.:

35.981 -120.546 (WGS84 datum and depth below mean sea level).

The rate of seismicity in the region local to SAFOD was particularly high during both Oct. (about 3 times normal) and Nov. (about 5-times normal).

Of the 256 events, 62 were blasts that could be located (all in Nov.). I have designated the blast events with an "*" in the last column of the event records.

The 2 most recent occurrences of the M2 target events were in Oct. (i.e., 031020 1125 and 031021 0900).

The catalog format (with C# designating column number) is:
C1  = Event ID
C2  = Origin time: Year (2-digits), Month, Day
C3  = Origin time: Hour, Minute
C4  = Origin time: Seconds
C5  = Lat., datum WGS84 
C6  = Lon., datum WGS84 
C7  = Depth (km below mean sea level).
C8  = Magnitude (HRSN magnitudes have not been determined and are set
	to 0.00. 57 of these events (including blasts) are also
	cataloged by the NCSN, and I have filled in the preferred
	magnitudes for these events in this column)
C9  = Number of P and S phases used in the locations.  Smaller events
	generally have fewer phases.
C10 = Rms residual of locations using Michelini  and McEvilly  (1991) P-
	and S- 3-D velocity
C11 = Events also in the NCSN catalog are designated "N", HRSN only are
	"-"
C12 = QB is a location quality designation.  Generally ~85% of the
	overall HRSN cataloged events are of QB quality, the rest are
	of lower quality.  QB quality events are generally more central
	to the network, have lower rms, and had depths that were not
	assigned to a default value of 1.0 km during the hypocentral
	inversion.
C13 = '*' designates a blast event.
